# European AI Infrastructure Market Notes

Working log for a market research pass on European AI infrastructure providers, including compute/data center operators, cloud and GPU platforms, model providers with deployment support, and consultancies / forward-deployed engineering style services.

## 2026-06-27

- Created project folder.
- Research questions:
  - Which company categories make up the European AI infrastructure market?
  - Who are the notable European providers in each category?
  - How are infrastructure, model, deployment, and services businesses structured?
  - Where might the market opportunity be for small and mid-sized enterprises adopting AI?
- Terminology: what the user called "server farms" is usually described as data centers, colocation facilities, hyperscale campuses, GPU clouds, AI factories, or AI gigafactories depending on scale and whether the facility includes accelerators and managed AI software.
- EU public infrastructure:
  - EuroHPC says Europe now has 19 AI Factories and 13 AI Factory Antennas offering free customised support to SMEs and startups.
  - EU AI Gigafactories are planned as large-scale facilities for next-generation models, with over 100,000 advanced AI processors and strong requirements around power, networking, supply chain, and efficiency.
  - InvestAI was announced to mobilise EUR 200B for AI, including EUR 20B for AI gigafactories.
- Physical data center operators:
  - DATA4 operates across France, Italy, Spain, Poland, Germany, and Greece; says it has 200ha land bank, 1.5GW available energy, and 21 additional data center buildings planned.
  - DATA4 announced a EUR 5B Northern France campus in Escaudain in June 2026 for AI growth.
  - atNorth is a pan-Nordic data center operator with sites in Iceland, Denmark, Sweden, Finland, and Norway; Partners Group announced sale to CPP Investments and Equinix at USD 4B EV in February 2026.
  - Green Mountain positions its Norway, UK, and Germany facilities for AI and HPC, emphasizing renewable energy and high-density infrastructure.
- GPU / AI cloud providers:
  - Scaleway offers European cloud and AI products including L4, L40S, H100, H100 SXM, and B300 SXM GPU instances; also markets dedicated GPU clusters for inference and sovereignty.
  - OVHcloud offers GPU cloud services and NVIDIA partnership positioning for deep learning, inference, and HPC.
  - Nebius is scaling aggressively: announced a 310MW AI factory in Lappeenranta, Finland, and UK deployments reaching 65MW when fully ramped in 2027.
  - Nscale positions as full-stack AI infrastructure "from ground to cloud" and announced a Microsoft deal for approximately 200,000 NVIDIA GB300 GPUs across Europe and the US.
  - Exoscale offers European GPU cloud, managed inference, vector databases, and dedicated NVIDIA GPUs.
  - IONOS documentation shows Cloud GPU VMs with single NVIDIA H200 GPUs; IONOS also launched Blackwell RTX PRO 6000 dedicated GPU servers in 2026.
  - Verda, formerly DataCrunch, is Finland-based and markets full-stack AI cloud, managed GPU fleets, and inference optimization; TNW reported a USD 117M raise in April 2026.
  - STACKIT, from Schwarz Group, positions as sovereign GDPR-compliant cloud and European hyperscaler; useful as a cloud/platform layer more than a pure GPU specialist from the sources checked.
- Model/platform companies moving into infrastructure and services:
  - Mistral Compute is explicitly a private integrated stack: GPUs, orchestration, APIs, products, and services, from bare metal to managed PaaS.
  - NVIDIA says Mistral AI is building an end-to-end cloud platform powered by 18,000 NVIDIA Grace Blackwell systems in the first phase, expanding across sites in 2026.
  - Mistral's own compute product page mentions 200MW sovereign capacity across the EU by 2027 and GB200 / GB300 / B300 generation hardware.
- Telco / industrial AI cloud:
  - Deutsche Telekom launched Industrial AI Cloud with NVIDIA and Polarise in Munich in February 2026; Telekom says it serves companies, research institutions, and the public sector with sovereign AI compute.
  - Earlier Telekom announcement said the infrastructure would enable large organizations, SMEs, and startups to develop, train, and use AI for manufacturing applications.
- Services / forward-deployed engineering:
  - Mistral Applied AI job descriptions show a forward-deployed style: designing, implementing, and deploying AI solutions for enterprise, critical infrastructure, and sovereign institutions.
  - Accenture and Mistral announced a multi-year strategic collaboration in February 2026 to help organizations scale advanced AI aligned with regional requirements.
  - Capgemini has a strategic partnership with Mistral focused on secure, responsible, scaled generative AI; Mistral publishes Capgemini as a self-hosted AI assistant customer case.
- Working conclusion:
  - The market is forming as a stack: land/power/cooling -> GPUs/network/storage -> cloud orchestration/inference APIs -> models/agents -> integration/governance/change management.
  - For SMEs, the likely bottleneck is less "access to a model" and more data readiness, integration into workflows, compliance, evaluation, cost controls, and ongoing operations.
